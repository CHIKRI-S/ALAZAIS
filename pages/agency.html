AGENCY

The Agency oversees operations conducted across known and derived universes.

The Agency is still young, yet it already possesses one of the most extensive histories, if not the most extensive, and one of the densest in all of Quanticity.

Human beings have always sought to understand the mysteries of their world. To do so, they founded countless institutions: scientific, religious, military, and at times even clandestine. For centuries, men and women devoted their entire lives to studying the unknown, observing its manifestations, classifying it, and sometimes attempting to master it.

Over time, certain organizations began to emerge on the margins of traditional structures. Secret foundations, paramilitary groups, and closed research circles all shared a common purpose: to focus their efforts on precise, identified phenomena considered significant enough to justify dedicated study programs.

It was in this context that the Alazaïs Agency came into existence, at the heart of a civilization in full expansion, too occupied with itself to perceive the strange events unfolding around it.

You may already have heard of such organizations. Through rumors, clandestinely circulated archives, uncertain testimonies, or theories dismissed as absurd.

These accounts are not entirely false. We exist.

In 1904, our founder, Professor Jean-Pierre Baltier, a former senior official of a secret foundation, left his position in order to devote himself to research of a more personal nature. His work concerned an ancient anomaly, barely visible within known archives, yet whose implications extended far beyond the limits of its time.
During that same year, the first significant discoveries were made near a device whose nature could not immediately be understood. Baltier, assisted by his wife Sophie Baltier and several scientific collaborators, then devoted months to the methodical study of this phenomenon, laying the first foundations of what would later become one of the largest research programs ever conducted by the Agency.

In July 1905, he officially founded Alazaïs, giving the emerging institution the name of his own lineage.

During the years that followed, the Agency continued its research with still limited resources, but with already considerable ambition. Progress was slow, at times uncertain, yet continuous. Each advance brought its teams closer to a new understanding of matter, space, and the mechanisms that connect certain worlds to one another.
On 8 September 1920, a major breakthrough was finally achieved. For the first time, a living being was successfully sent beyond an experimental threshold. This event marked a decisive turning point in the Agency’s history, opening the way to decades of exploration, classification, and expansion.

From that moment onward, Alazaïs changed scale. Its infrastructure expanded, its research was supported by new resources, and several state institutions authorized the continuation of studies that had until then been unimaginable.
One condition, however, was imposed from the earliest years of this cooperation: the very existence of such work was never to be made known to the public.
That rule was respected for decades.

Since then, the Agency has never ceased to extend its field of action. It has explored new worlds, studied unknown phenomena, observed entire civilizations, secured sites, assembled archives of unparalleled scale, and developed structures capable of sustaining operations across multiple realities.

The Agency is still young when measured against what it studies. And yet, it already possesses one of the most extensive histories, if not the most extensive, and one of the densest in all of Quanticity.

And its official history represents only a part of it.
